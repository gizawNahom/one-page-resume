My Story

Hi! My name is Nahom, and I am a software craftsman who builds for the web. I started programming at AAIT(Addis Ababa Institute of Technology)--where I studied software engineering--in one of the first courses I took. It was a CS50-style course that covered the fundamental computer science concepts and skills needed to write a full-stack web app. It was overwhelming at the time, but in hindsight, it gave me the drive to independently explore my curiosity. In a subsequent course, I came across Extreme Programming(XP). It had practices like Test Driven Development(TDD) and Pair Programming that fascinated me. I scoured the internet for material and found mentors like Robert C. Martin(Uncle Bob), Martin Fowler and Eric Evans. I learned a lot about the practices, teams and processes that make great software, especially from Uncle Bob. He spoke about the programmer’s oath, and I took it to heart. He's the reason I call myself a software craftsman: a professional who strives for maximum quality, accountability and integrity.

I started my career, as an intern, at a software company called Minab. At Minab, I designed and developed full-stack apps together with my teammates. After graduation, I worked, for a short time, at a small Fintech startup called Zeraf, where I turned a vision into a workable User Story Map. I then went into CNET--the largest ERP software provider in Ethiopia--where I honed most of my skills. I met with clients; gathered their requirements; designed, developed and deployed numerous projects. Working for CNET gave me practical knowledge of Pair Programming, TDD, Design Patterns and SOLID principles. It also enhanced my communication, collaboration and leadership skills.

Here are some of the skills I’ve acquired over the years(complete list on my resume):

  • .NET
  • Node.js
  • REST APIs
  • MongoDB
  • Docker
  • CI/CD
  • Vue
  • Javascript(ES6+)
  • C#
  • Express.js
